# AI-Generated Content in 2024: Risks, Rewards, and Safe Strategies

Google's AI Overviews mean AI-written content faces new scrutiny. But done right, it still works. The sites winning in 2024 aren't avoiding AI — they're using it strategically, with real human expertise layered on top.

Here's how to use AI content safely with your Search Console data as your guide.

## Step 1: Know When AI Content Works Best

AI excels at:

- Drafting outlines, data summaries, and step-by-step guides
- Generating multiple title and meta description variations quickly
- Creating FAQ sections based on real search queries
- Updating evergreen content with new statistics

AI struggles with:

- Original insights and first-hand experience
- Complex, nuanced opinions
- Content where trust signals and authority matter most (YMYL topics)

Check [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console): pages with AI-generated drafts often rank well for long-tail informational queries, but underperform for high-intent commercial searches where users need to trust the source.

[SerpDo's Article Ideas](/article-ideas) generates topic suggestions based on your actual keyword gaps — a smarter starting point than asking AI to guess what your site needs.

## Step 2: Always Add the Human Layer

Never publish raw AI output. Use it as a starting point, then:

- Add real examples from your experience or clients
- Include recent data, statistics, or case studies
- Rewrite sections to match your brand voice and tone
- Add original perspective that AI cannot replicate

Use this prompt to improve AI drafts:

```
This AI draft explains "[topic]" generically.
Add two specific examples from [industry] and cite a recent source or statistic.
Rewrite the conclusion to include a clear, opinionated recommendation.
```

Google's [quality rater guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) prioritize E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Raw AI output typically lacks the "Experience" dimension.

## Step 3: Measure Performance Rigorously

Track two metrics in [Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console) for every AI-assisted page:

- **Average position** — is Google ranking it competitively?
- **Click-through rate** — are users choosing it over competitors?

If rankings rise but CTR stays flat, you're visible but not compelling. Use [Content Refresh](/content-refresh) to generate AI-improved title tags and meta descriptions based on your real query data.

[SerpDo's Opportunities page](/opportunities) automatically flags pages where CTR lags behind potential, so you know exactly where to focus.

## Real Example

A client used AI to draft 20 blog posts. The ones with added case studies and updated statistics — after 30 days, 15 ranked in the top 3. The purely AI-written pages without human enrichment stayed on page 2.

The difference wasn't topic selection or keyword targeting. It was the human layer.

## Key Takeaways

- Use AI for structure and speed, not for final copy
- Always inject human expertise, real examples, and recent data
- Track both rankings and CTR in Search Console — both matter
- If CTR lags after ranking, fix the title and meta description first
- [Google's helpful content system](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) rewards demonstrable expertise
- AI + human review is the only sustainable 2024 content strategy
