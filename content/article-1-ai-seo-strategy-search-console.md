# How to Build an AI SEO Strategy Using Google Search Console Data

Your Search Console is full of untapped potential. Most people ignore the data sitting right there. They chase tactics instead of asking what Google is already telling them.

Here's how to turn those numbers into winning SEO moves using AI. This isn't about replacing your brain. It's about speeding up what you already do.

## Step 1: Pull Your Performance Data

Open [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console) and click **Performance**. Export the last 90 days of data as CSV. Look for pages with declining click-through rates. These are your quick wins.

Why? Google gave you the data. You just need to read it.

Tools like [SerpDo's Opportunities page](/opportunities) automatically surface these low-CTR pages ranked by impact — no spreadsheet digging required.

## Step 2: Ask Better Questions with AI Prompts

Don't just ask "improve SEO." That gets you generic advice. Instead, paste this into your favorite AI tool:

```
Page X gets 500 monthly visits but 2% CTR. The title tag is "Best Coffee Makers 2023"
and meta description reads "Top coffee makers for home use."
Write 3 new title options and meta descriptions focusing on [specific keyword] to boost clicks.
```

This prompt works because it's specific. It ties the problem to actual numbers.

SerpDo's [AI Coach](/coach) does this automatically — it knows your site's real GSC data and answers "why is my CTR dropping?" with real, data-backed recommendations.

## Step 3: Rewrite Based on Real Data

Your AI response gives you options. Pick one. Test it. Watch Search Console for 7–14 days.

This cycle works. You're not guessing. You're responding to what users actually search and click.

Use [Content Refresh](/content-refresh) to get AI-drafted title tags and meta descriptions for any page, pre-enriched with your actual GSC query data.

## Real Example

A client had a page about "SEO tools." CTR sat at 1.8%. We used this exact process. New titles focused on "free SEO tools" and "rank tracking software." CTR jumped to 4.2%. Traffic followed.

Results beat theory every time.
