# AI Content Gap Analysis: Fix Low-Performing Pages in 3 Steps

Your Search Console shows pages that get seen but not clicked. These are your content gap opportunities. They're already indexed, already ranking — they just need the right adjustment to convert impressions into traffic. AI can help you find and fix them fast.

## Step 1: Identify Content Gaps

Export your top 20 pages from [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console). Sort by impressions, but filter for low CTR (under 3%). These pages are getting seen — just not clicked.

Use this AI prompt to diagnose the issue:

```
Page at [URL] gets [X] impressions but only [Y]% CTR. The title is "[current title]"
and the topic is [keyword]. What content gaps exist that could improve click-through rates?
What does the user want to see that my title and meta description aren't promising?
```

[SerpDo's Opportunities page](/opportunities) does this automatically — it flags your low-CTR pages sorted by potential impact, so you know exactly where to start.

## Step 2: Map User Intent to Better Content

Low CTR usually means **mismatched intent**. The searcher expects one thing; your title promises another.

Common gaps:

| What users want | What the page delivers |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Free tools/templates | Paid enterprise solutions |
| Step-by-step guide | High-level overview |
| Local results | Generic national content |
| 2024/2025 info | Outdated statistics |

Use this AI prompt to realign:

```
Users searching "[keyword]" want to [specific goal].
My current title is "[title]" and meta description is "[description]".
Rewrite both to match actual user intent, using concrete benefits and specifics.
```

Then use [Content Refresh](/content-refresh) to get AI-generated title tags and meta descriptions pre-loaded with your actual search data.

## Step 3: Implement and Measure

Apply the AI suggestions. Track CTR changes in Search Console over 2–3 weeks — most meaningful improvements show up within that window.

Also run a [Page Grade](/page-grader) before and after to measure overall SEO impact beyond just CTR.

## Real Example

A client's page about "SEO tools" had 10,000 monthly impressions but only 2% CTR. The title promised a free tool list but the content focused on paid enterprise solutions — a classic intent mismatch.

Fix: Changed title to "Top 10 Free SEO Tools for Small Businesses" and updated the intro to address the free-tool audience. CTR rose from 2% to 5.8% within 14 days — a 3× lift without rewriting the whole article.
